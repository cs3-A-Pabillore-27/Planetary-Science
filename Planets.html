What are Planets?

  • A more modern definition can be found in the Merriam-Webster dictionary which defines a planet as "any of the large bodies that revolve around the Sun in the solar system."

How many planets does our Solar system have?

We have 9 planets in total which are:






Mercury: this planet is the smallest planet, though the nearest it is the 2nd hottest planet in our system.

Venus: 2nd row away from the Sun, this planet is considered the hottest planet despite Mercury being the closest to the sun.

Earth:3rd row away from the Sun, our current home where the only known lifeform are seen.

Mars: 4th row away from the Sun, also called "The Red Planet" this planet is the brightest on Earth's sky due to its reflective surfaces.

Jupiter: 5th row away from the Sun, this planet is largest and oldest in our Solar system.

Saturn: 6th row away from the Sun, known for its beutiful rings, and does rings are made of ice, rocks, dust particles. A gas giant being the 2nd largest planet in our system.

Uranus: 7 row away from the Sun, an Ice giant composing of hydrogen, helium, ices textured water, and its faint rings with many moons.

Neptune:Farthest planet away from the sun, same traits as Uranus, this planet has the strongest winds in our system.

Pluto: Used to be considered a planet, until 2006 the International Astronomical Unit(IAU) considered it a dwarf planet, they didn't considered it as a planet due to it not meeting one criteria which is debris not cleared from its orbit.
